Colon Theatre

Built in 1857 and moved in 1908, the Colon theatre is an old theatre located in Buenos Aires, Argentina where people can go listen to music, watch plays, or watch people dancing. If you do not like any of those things, visiting the Colon Theatre is still highly recommended because of its impressive architecture.

Japanese Gardens

Going a little bit off the Argentinean culture, the Japanese Gardens is a wonderful place to go for a walk or just spend some time relaxing. This place is extremely attractive during the spring season when all of the flowers start to bloom and color the whole gardens with different colors. In order to go to walk through the gardens, you will have to walk across some red bridges that represent the way from life to a better place.

Recoleta

Recoleta is a city from Buenos Aires where many people enjoy some time walking through its streets. Having places with European architecture, Recoleta, is one of the most visited cities in the province. It also has a cementry that attracts people from all over the world with its unique tombstones from famous Argentinean people.

Caminito - La Boca

If you are in Argentina, you cannot miss going to Caminito, La Boca, the most colorful place you will see. All of the houses in this place are painted different colors and decorated with the typical Argentinean painting technique called Fileteado. This place is perfect to learn about the traditions from Buenos Aires, including Tango and Fileteado. This part of the city is recommended to go during the day to just wander around because you will have an awesome time with the street artists who paint, dance, sing, or play an instrument in the streets giving a show at any time.

Galileo Galilei Planetarium

The planetarium Galileo Galilei is a place for everyone. This incredible place has different science exhibits each month and is surrounded by a huge park with artificial lakes. People go to these lakes to have a picnic or to relax after watching shows about space and other science things. If you have time, you should also stay until nighttime since the planetarium offers amazing night activities.

Plaza De Mayo

Surrounded by the Casa Rosada (the government house), the national congress, national banks, the Cabildo and many other historical buildings, Plaza de Mayo is an incredible place to go spend some time and learn about the history. You can also book tours in advance around the different buildings to get the full experience. Plaza de Mayo, translated as Park of May, is also a place to go spend some time. Since it is located in the middle of the capital city of Buenos Aires province, you will never get bored at this place.

Obelisk

In the middle of the wildest street in the world, you will find the Obelisk, the symbol that represents the province of Buenos Aires and the entire country of Argentina. This building is a place that you must see on your visit to Argentina. It is recommended to go there during the day to take pictures and walk a little bit, but if you want to get the real experience, it is recommended to go during the night and find about the night activities that this place offers. Finally, if you are lucky, you will be able to go there when the country is celebrating something because that is when the big parties happen, such as the one in the picture, where around five million Argentineans met to celebrate the world cup in 2022.
